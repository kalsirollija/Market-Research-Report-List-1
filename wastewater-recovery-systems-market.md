<p><p>Wastewater recovery systems encompass various filtration and purification technologies. Reverse osmosis employs semi-permeable membranes to remove contaminants by applying pressure, producing high-quality water. Membrane filtration utilizes different membrane types for separating particles and microorganisms. Activated carbon systems adsorb organic compounds and chemicals, enhancing water taste and odor. Media filtration employs layers of material, like sand or gravel, to trap impurities and suspended solids. Together, these technologies contribute to efficient wastewater treatment and sustainable water reuse.</p></p>
