<p><ul><li>Anionic Surfactant</li><li>Cationic Surfactant</li><li>Amphoteric Surfactant</li><li>Nonionic Surfactant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The surfactant market is categorized into four main types: anionic, cationic, amphoteric, and nonionic surfactants. Anionic surfactants, known for their strong cleaning abilities, are widely used in detergents. Cationic surfactants provide antimicrobial properties and are utilized in fabric softeners and personal care products. Amphoteric surfactants possess both positive and negative charges, making them versatile for various applications, including shampoos. Nonionic surfactants, which are less sensitive to pH changes, are commonly found in industrial and household cleaners.</p></p>
