<p><ul><li>Natural Convection</li><li>Forced Convection</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Laboratory Universal Oven market is divided into two primary types: Natural Convection and Forced Convection ovens. Natural Convection ovens rely on the natural movement of heated air, where hot air rises and cool air descends, providing gentle temperature distribution. In contrast, Forced Convection ovens utilize fans to circulate air, enabling faster and more uniform heating. Both types serve various laboratory applications, ensuring precise control of temperature for sterilization, drying, or baking processes, catering to specific laboratory needs and efficiency requirements.</p></p>
